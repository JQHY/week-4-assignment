Cool Facts About Blockchain

What is a blockchain?

Blockchain is a distributed, decentralised public ledger. This means:

  1. Distributed - every user of the ledger has a copy of it; no one server stores transactions
  2. Decentralised - blockchain has no central regulation body, instead transactions are verified by the network of computers
  3. Public - anyone can access the ledger and make transactions

This website has a very clear and comprehensive guide if you want to learn more.

Fun Fact!

Ethereum enthusiasts launched CryptoKitties to encourage new users to try out blockchain, but they didn't quite expect the scheme to be as popular as it was...

It allows you to breed your own genetically unique feline friends and buy cats from a marketplace for an average price of $20 USD. Each cat has its own combination of traits, named "cattributes", with the most desirable selling for up to $114,000!

When it was first launched in December 2017, it crashed many Ethereum transactions, many of which were genuine business ones, due to the hugely increased demand. At one point as much as 10% of all Ethereum transactions were thanks to the addictive CryptoKitties!


What impact could blockchain have in the future?

There are still many possible uses of blockchain being explored across all disciplines, and it has a lot of potential to make a significant impact on life as we know it today:

  • Crypto Banks are predicted to overtake traditional ones, with many already starting to expand their services to allow their customers to use cryptocurrencies
  • Systems to allow voting by blockchain are being developed, with many believing they could be the future preferred method
  • It has the potential to transform healthcare

And there are many more groundbreaking applications on the way!
